Problem: A child builds a sandcastle in a sandbox. The sandbox has the shape of a cubic box with 512 cubic feet of sand. What is the length of each side of the sandbox in feet?

Response: 

To find the length of each side of the cubic sandbox, we need to determine the cube root of the volume. The volume of the sandbox is given as 512 cubic feet.

The formula for the volume \( V \) of a cube with side length \( s \) is:
\[ V = s^3 \]

Given \( V = 512 \) cubic feet, we need to solve for \( s \):
\[ s^3 = 512 \]

To find \( s \), we take the cube root of both sides:
\[ s = \sqrt[3]{512} \]

We can determine the cube root of 512 by recognizing that:
\[ 512 = 8 \times 8 \times 8 = 8^3 \]

Thus:
\[ \sqrt[3]{512} = 8 \]

Therefore, the length of each side of the sandbox is 8 feet.
