Problem: A child builds a sandcastle in a sandbox. The sandbox has the shape of a cubic box with 512 cubic feet of sand. What is the length of each side of the sandbox in feet?

Response: 

Since the sandbox is a cubic box, all sides have the same length. Let's call this length "s". The volume of the sandbox is given as 512 cubic feet, which is equal to the product of the length, width, and height of the box. Since it's a cube, the length, width, and height are all equal to "s". So, we can set up the equation:

s × s × s = 512

This is a cubic equation, and we can solve for "s" by taking the cube root of both sides:

s = ∛512

s = 8

So, the length of each side of the sandbox is 8 feet.
