Problem: A mountain climber starts at a point that is m feet high. After climbing downwards for a while, they reach a point that's 10 feet high. If they had climbed down 4 feet to reach this point, how high was the point where they started?

Response:

1. The mountain climber starts at a point that is m feet high.
2. They climb down to a point that is 10 feet high. This means they have descended m - 10 feet from the starting point.
3. At this point, they have climbed down 4 feet further to reach the 10 feet high point. This means they have descended an additional 4 feet from the previous point.
4. So, the total height they have descended from the starting point is m - 10 - 4 = m - 14 feet.
5. Since they have descended this height, the starting point must be 14 feet higher than the 10 feet high point. Therefore, the starting point is 10 + 14 = m feet high.
