Solve real world and mathematical problems involving perimeters of polygons, including finding the perimeter given the side lengths, finding an unknown side length, and exhibiting rectangles with the same perimeter and different areas or with the same area and different perimeters.

Problem: An equilateral triangle has a perimeter of 990cm. What is the length of each of its sides?

Gemma 2B: 

Reasoning: Let's call the length of each side of the equilateral triangle "x". Since it's an equilateral triangle, all three sides have the same length. The perimeter of the triangle is the sum of the lengths of all three sides, which is 3x.

We are given that the perimeter is 990cm, so we can set up the equation:

3x = 990

To solve for x, we can divide both sides of the equation by 3:

x = 990/3
x = 330

So, the length of each side of the equilateral triangle is 330cm.

### Answer: 330

Problem: A triangle has a perimeter of 997m. Two of its sides have lengths of 2m and 667m. What is the length of the third side?

Perimeter = a + b + c

We know the perimeter is 997m, and we know two of the sides have lengths 2m and 667m. So, we can set up the equation:

997 = 2 + 667 + c

So, the length of the third side is 328m.
