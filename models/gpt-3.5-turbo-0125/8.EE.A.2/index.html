Gemma 2B: 

Given that the volume of one page is 64 cubic inches, and the volume of a cube is given by the formula \( \text{Volume} = \text{Side Length}^3 \), we can set up the equation \( 64 = \text{Side Length}^3 \) and solve for the side length.

Taking the cube root of both sides, we get \( \text{Side Length} = \sqrt[3]{64} = 4 \) inches.

### Answer: \( \text{Side Length} = 4 \) inches.
