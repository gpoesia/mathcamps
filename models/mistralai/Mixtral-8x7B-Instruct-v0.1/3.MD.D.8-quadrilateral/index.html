Width = (324 - 166) / 2

Problem: A rectangle has a perimeter of 52 meters. If one of the sides measures 4 meters, how long is the other side?

Mixtral 8x7B: 

 Reasoning: The perimeter of a rectangle is calculated by adding all its sides. This means that the perimeter of the rectangle is 4 meters + 4 meters + length of the other side + length of the other side.

We know that the perimeter is 52 meters, so:

4 meters + 4 meters + length of the other side + length of the other side = 52 meters

Let's call the length of the other side "x". Then, the equation becomes:

8 + 2x = 52

Subtract 8 from both sides:

2x = 44

Divide both sides by 2:

x = 22

### Answer: The other side is 22 meters long.
