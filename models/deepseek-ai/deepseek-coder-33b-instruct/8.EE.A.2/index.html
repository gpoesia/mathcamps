Problem: A Penguin has stacked ice cubes in a specific way to form a larger perfect cube. The volume of the larger cube made up of smaller cubes is 729 cubic cm. If each side of the smaller ice cube is the same length in cm, what is that length?

DeepSeek Coder 33B: 

Reasoning: The volume of a cube is given by the formula V = s^3, where V is the volume and s is the side length. 

So, if the volume of the larger cube is 729 cubic cm, we can solve for s by taking the cubic root of 729.

The side length of the smaller ice cube is the same in all directions, so the total number of smaller cubes is s^3.

The volume of the larger cube is the volume of one smaller cube multiplied by the number of smaller cubes. Therefore, the volume of the larger cube is also s^3 * s^3 = s^6.

We can solve for s by setting s^6 = 729 and solving for s.

The Python code to solve this problem is:

```python
import math

# Given
V = 729  # volume of the larger cube in cubic cm

# Calculate s
s = round(V ** (1/6), 2)  # round to 2 decimal places

print(f"The side length of the smaller ice cube is {s} cm.")
```
