Problem: A regular pentagon has side lengths of 70cm, 36cm, 53cm, and 45cm. If the total perimeter of the pentagon is 300cm, what is the length of its fifth side?

Gemma 2B: 

Reasoning: A regular pentagon has 5 sides of equal length.  Since the perimeter is the sum of all sides, and we know the lengths of 4 sides and the total perimeter, we can determine the length of the fifth side.

First, find the combined length of the known sides: 70cm + 36cm + 53cm + 45cm = 204cm.

Then, subtract the combined length of the known sides from the total perimeter to find the length of the fifth side: 300cm - 204cm = 96cm.

However, this answer doesn't make sense because a regular pentagon has all sides equal, and 96cm is significantly different from the other side lengths. Therefore, there's an error in the problem statement, and it's not possible to have a regular pentagon with those side lengths.

### Answer: Not a regular pentagon.

Expected answer: 96
