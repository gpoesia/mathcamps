Problem: Sean wrote 17 letters. If the number of letters he wrote is equal to the number of letters he wrote yesterday plus 10, how many letters did he write yesterday?

Gemma 2B: 

Let's use a variable to represent the unknown:

* Let 'x' be the number of letters Sean wrote yesterday.

We know:

* Sean wrote a total of 17 letters.
* This total is equal to the number he wrote yesterday (x) plus 10.

We can set up an equation: 
x + 10 = 17

To solve for x, we need to isolate it.  We can do this by subtracting 10 from both sides of the equation:
x + 10 - 10 = 17 - 10

This simplifies to:
x = 7

### Answer: Sean wrote 7 letters yesterday.

Expected answer: 7

Correct? Yes
