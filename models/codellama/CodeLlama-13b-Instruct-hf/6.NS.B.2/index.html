Problem: There are 73841 cubic centimeters of air needed to inflate a large beach ball. If we fill the ball up at a rate of 41 cubic centimeters per second, how many seconds will it take to fully inflate the beach ball?

CodeLlama 13B: 

  Reasoning: To find out how many seconds it will take to fully inflate the beach ball, we need to divide the total volume of air needed to inflate the ball by the rate at which the ball is being filled.

Total volume of air needed to inflate the ball = 73841 cubic centimeters
Rate at which the ball is being filled = 41 cubic centimeters per second

To find out how many seconds it will take to fully inflate the ball, we can divide the total volume of air needed to inflate the ball by the rate at which the ball is being filled:

Total time = Total volume of air needed to inflate the ball / Rate at which the ball is being filled
Total time = 73841 cubic centimeters / 41 cubic centimeters per second
Total time = 1797.5 seconds

Therefore, it will take approximately 1797.5 seconds or 29 minutes and 57.5 seconds to fully inflate the beach ball at a rate of 41 cubic centimeters per second.

### Answer: 1797.5 seconds
