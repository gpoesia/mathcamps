Problem: A rectangle has a length of 21m and a width of 37m. What is the perimeter of the rectangle?

CodeLlama 34B: 

  To find the perimeter of a rectangle, we need to add up the lengths of all its sides. The perimeter of a rectangle is equal to the sum of the lengths of its two sides that are parallel to each other, plus the sum of the lengths of its two sides that are perpendicular to each other.

In this case, the length of the rectangle is 21m, and the width is 37m. The perimeter of the rectangle is therefore:

21 + 21 + 37 + 37 = 116m

So the perimeter of the rectangle is 116m.

Expected answer: 116

Correct? Yes
