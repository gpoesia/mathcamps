Problem: In their game, Kyle threw a ball for a distance of 18/4 yards, while Lisa threw the same ball for a distance of 19/9 yards. How many yards did the ball travel in total?

Gemma 2B: 

  To find the total distance traveled by the ball, we need to find the ratio of the distances traveled by Kyle and Lisa.

Let's say the ratio of the distances traveled by Kyle and Lisa is x. Then, we can write:

18/4 = x \* 19/9

Simplifying the equation, we get:

18 = x \* 19

Now, we can divide both sides by 19 to solve for x:

x = 18/19

So, the ratio of the distances traveled by Kyle and Lisa is 18/19.

Now, we can find the total distance traveled by the ball by multiplying the ratio by the total distance:

Total distance = Ratio \* Total distance
Total distance = 18/19 \* (18/4 + 19/9)
Total distance = 18/19 \* 21/4
Total distance = 18/19 \* 5
Total distance = 9

Therefore, the ball traveled a total distance of 9 yards.
