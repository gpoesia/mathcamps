Problem: A rectangle has a length of 21m and a width of 37m. What is the perimeter of the rectangle?

Response: 

To find the perimeter of a rectangle, we use the formula:

\[ \text{Perimeter} = 2 \times (\text{Length} + \text{Width}) \]

Given:
- Length (\(L\)) = 21 meters
- Width (\(W\)) = 37 meters

First, we add the length and the width:

\[ L + W = 21 + 37 = 58 \]

Next, we multiply the sum by 2 to find the perimeter:

\[ \text{Perimeter} = 2 \times 58 = 116 \]

### Answer: 116 meters
