Problem: At a party, 66 teenagers and 6 adults attend at first. Later on, the number of guests increases by 62. Near the end, an additional group of 52 people join the party. How many people attended the party in total?

Gemma 2B: 

To solve this problem, we need to find the sum of the initial number of guests and the additional guests that joined later.

Given information:
- Initially, there were 66 teenagers and 6 adults attending the party.
- Later on, the number of guests increased by 62.
- Near the end, an additional group of 52 people joined the party.

Step 1: Find the initial number of guests.
Initial number of guests = Number of teenagers + Number of adults
Initial number of guests = 66 + 6 = 72 people

Step 2: Find the total number of additional guests.
Total additional guests = Guests who joined later + Additional group near the end
Total additional guests = 62 + 52 = 114 people

Step 3: Find the total number of people who attended the party.
Total number of people = Initial number of guests + Total additional guests
Total number of people = 72 + 114 = 186 people

Therefore, 186 people attended the party in total.
