Problem: A chocolate factory initially had 33 chocolate bars. However, 6 of them were given away as samples to customers. Then, 3 of them were used for a quality control testing. How many chocolate bars are left in the factory?

Gemma 2B: 

To solve this problem, we need to find the total number of chocolate bars that were taken away from the initial stock, and then subtract it from the initial stock.

Given information:
- The factory initially had 33 chocolate bars.
- 6 chocolate bars were given away as samples to customers.
- 3 chocolate bars were used for quality control testing.

Step 1: Find the total number of chocolate bars taken away.
Number of chocolate bars given away as samples + Number of chocolate bars used for quality control testing = Total number of chocolate bars taken away
6 + 3 = 9 chocolate bars taken away
