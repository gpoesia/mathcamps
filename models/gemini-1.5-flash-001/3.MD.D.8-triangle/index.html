Problem: A triangle has a perimeter of 762 meters. Two sides of these triangle are known to be 122 meters and 245 meters long respectively. Can you calculate the length of the third side?

Response: 

Here's how to calculate the length of the third side:

1. **Perimeter Formula:** The perimeter of a triangle is the sum of the lengths of all its sides. So, Perimeter = side1 + side2 + side3.

2. **Substitute Known Values:** We know the perimeter (762 meters) and two sides (122 meters and 245 meters).  Let the unknown side be represented by 'x'. 
   Therefore, 762 = 122 + 245 + x

3. **Solve for 'x':**
   * Combine the known side lengths: 762 = 367 + x
   * Subtract 367 from both sides: 762 - 367 = x
   * Calculate the result: 395 = x

Therefore, the length of the third side of the triangle is 395 meters.
