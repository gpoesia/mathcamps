Problem: The sun is at an angle of 'j' degrees above the horizon. If we decrease this angle by 24 degrees, it becomes 52 degrees. What was the original angle of the sun above the horizon?

Gemini-1.5 Flash: 

Here's how we can solve this problem:

* **Let 'j' represent the original angle.**
* **We know that decreasing the angle by 24 degrees results in 52 degrees.**  This can be written as an equation: j - 24 = 52
* **To solve for 'j', we need to isolate it. Add 24 to both sides of the equation:** j - 24 + 24 = 52 + 24
* **This simplifies to:** j = 76

Therefore, the original angle of the sun above the horizon was 76 degrees.

### Answer: 76 degrees
