Reasoning: We know that John had 10 strings initially and is left with (e + 6) strings after some break.  This means the number of broken strings is the difference between the initial number and the remaining number of strings.

So, the number of broken strings is 10 - (e + 6) = 10 - e - 6 = 4 - e.

Since the number of broken strings is also represented by 'e', we have:

4 - e = e

Adding 'e' to both sides: 4 = 2e

Dividing both sides by 2: e = 2

Therefore, the value of 'e' is 2.
