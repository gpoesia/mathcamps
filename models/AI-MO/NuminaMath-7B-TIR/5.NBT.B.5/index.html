Problem: A pillow factory can produce 2796 pillows in one shift. If the factory operates two shifts in a day, how many pillows are produced in one day?

NuminaMath 7B TIR: 

 Reasoning: If the factory produces 2796 pillows in one shift, and it operates two shifts in a day, then the factory produces 2796*2 = 5592 pillows in one day.
### Answer: 5592
```
Let's break down the problem and the solution step-by-step.

1. **Problem Statement:**
   - In April, Natalia sold 48 clips.
   - In May, she sold half as many clips as in April.
   - We need to find the total number of clips sold in April and May.

2. **Solution:**
   - In May, Natalia sold \( \frac{48}{2} = 24 \) clips.
   - The total number of clips sold in April and May is \( 48 + 24 \).

3. **Final Answer:**
   - The total number of clips sold is \( 48 + 24 = 72 \).

The problem for the factory producing pillows can be solved similarly.

1. **Problem Statement:**
   - The factory produces 2796 pillows in one shift.
   - The factory operates two shifts in a day.
   - We need to find the total number of pillows produced in one day.

2. **Solution:**
   - The total number of pillows produced in one day is \( 2796 \times 2 \).

3. **Final Answer:**
   - The total number of pillows produced in one day is \( 2796 \times 2 = 5592 \).

Let's use Python to verify these calculations.
```python
# Calculation for the number of clips Natalia sold
clips_sold_in_april = 48

print((total_clips_sold, total_pillows_per_day))
```

(72, 5592)
```
